New Orleans-Style Red Beans and Rice

Prep: 20 min Stovetop: 2.5–3 hrs Slow Cooker: 7–8 hrs
⬇ Download PDF

"A true Louisiana staple. Perfectly creamy, deeply savory red beans simmered low and slow with the holy trinity of vegetables, a smoky ham hock, and deeply browned Andouille sausage."

Ingredients

Aromatics & Meat

  • 1 lb dried red kidney beans, rinsed and picked over
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or lard
  • 1 lb Andouille sausage, sliced into ¼-inch rounds
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely diced
  • 3 ribs celery, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 smoked ham hock (or meaty ham bone)

Simmer & Seasoning

  • 6 cups chicken stock (stovetop) or 4½–5 cups (slow cooker)
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp Cajun or Creole seasoning
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper (adjust for heat)
  • 1 tsp hot sauce (Crystal or Tabasco)
  • Kosher salt and black pepper to taste

For Serving

  • Cooked long-grain white rice
  • Fresh scallions, thinly sliced
  • Extra hot sauce for the table

Stovetop Instructions

  1. Soak the beans: Place dried red beans in a large bowl and cover with water by at least 2 inches. Soak overnight (or at least 8 hours). Drain and rinse before cooking.
  2. Brown the sausage: Heat oil or lard in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add sliced Andouille and cook until beautifully browned (5–7 minutes). Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the fat in the pot.
  3. Sauté the Holy Trinity: Turn heat to medium. Add the onion, bell pepper, and celery and cook, stirring occasionally, for 8–10 minutes until soft and translucent. Stir in the garlic and cook 1 more minute until fragrant.
  4. Low and slow simmer: Add the drained beans, chicken stock, ham hock, bay leaves, thyme, Cajun seasoning, and cayenne. Bring to a rolling boil, then lower to medium-low. Cover and simmer 1½–2 hours until beans are tender.
  5. Create the creaminess: Return the browned sausage to the pot. Mash a portion of the beans against the side of the Dutch oven with a wooden spoon. Stir well and simmer uncovered for 30 more minutes until the liquid thickens into a rich gravy. Stir in hot sauce and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Plate and serve: Remove and discard bay leaves and the ham hock bone (shred any remaining meat back into the pot). Spoon creamy red beans over warm white rice and garnish with sliced scallions. Serve with extra hot sauce.

Slow Cooker Conversion

  1. Soak & pre-boil: Soak beans overnight. Boil soaked beans in fresh water for 10 minutes to destroy toxins, then drain before adding to the slow cooker.
  2. Sauté base: Brown the sausage and sauté the Holy Trinity with garlic in a skillet on the stove as above. Transfer everything into the slow cooker basin.
  3. Load basin: Add pre-boiled beans, sautéed vegetables, ham hock, bay leaves, thyme, Cajun seasoning, and cayenne. Pour in only 4½–5 cups of chicken stock. Hold back the sausage and hot sauce until the end.
  4. Cook: Cover and cook on LOW for 7–8 hours (preferred) or HIGH for 4–5 hours.
  5. Finish: 30 minutes before done, stir in the reserved sausage. Mash some beans against the crock wall for the signature thick gravy. Discard bay leaves, shred ham hock meat back in, stir in hot sauce, and serve over warm white rice.
